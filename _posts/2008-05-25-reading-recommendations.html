I would say I'd probably spend most of my time reading, ( well maybe onine, but I do read a lot).

My kids are starting to pick the habit, and I think there is a small gap between kids books ( a lot of drawings , few words), and the likes of Harry Potter ( hundreds of pages and not a single drawing).

Kids will fill this naturally in one way or another , but I often find that short stories ( especially fantasy) is one of them, the classic short novels are sometimes adapted for old kids in a condensed version with drawings ( R.L Stevenson, and Emilio Salgari were my favorites when I was a kid ) and the final and more fun : comics books . Not any comic will do here you mus familiarize with the genius of Rene√© Goscinny , his work is interesting, good, story and full of sunny historical (and other) references. I especially recommend Asterix and The Adventures of TinTin as the pinnacle of this kind of comic/novel, with the Lucky Luke series close behind. I don't know a single person that has read those as a kid that don't have their own collection...

Important: Avoid the latest Asterix books as they are not written by the great Goscinny they should have never been published....a shame.
