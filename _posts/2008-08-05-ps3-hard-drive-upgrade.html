--- layout: post title: PS3 Hard Drive Upgrade date: '2008-08-05T16:28:00.013-03:00' author: Alejandro tags: - ps3 - games - gadgets - playstation modified_time: '2008-12-09T22:24:55.418-02:00' thumbnail: http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_12j-6WCLPuM/SJisH-ksOYI/AAAAAAAAAIA/5d4fdA74_PU/s72-c/Photo-0096.jpg blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-3848561.post-6730399181889986094 blogger_orig_url: http://alepanizza.blogspot.com/2008/08/ps3-hard-drive-upgrade.html --- Having a good music and video collection, my trusty old 60 Gb PS3 was at his top capacity; and buying a webcam (PS Eye ), and Singstar, didn't improve things... So I decided to upgrade; after a bit of research I settled for a new WD 320 GB Scorpio disk ( amazon ).

The only important thing to remember is that you need a 2.5'' (notebook) SATA Hard Drive. The one that comes with the machine is 5400 rpm, so it is best to use the same speed also. I am not aware that there is any limit in the supported capacity of the PS3 drive, but maybe it is.
In any case 320 GB is recognized and works.

I checked some sites on "how to" and decided it would be really easy :-)

First rule : Backup

The PS3 has a backup utility that backs up almost everything: other stuff like usernames and configs and the system software is not in the hard disk .
I had to delete some stuff ( I had copies of almost all of it) to make the backup fit in my ipod.

Remember to turn everything off and unplug the console to avoid problems.

Opening the HD door, It's just removing a plastic panel, no big deal , you can use a flat screwdriver or a knife :-)
you get this:

That is the hard drive, after removing the center "blue screw" you pull right, and then out, the metal handle and remove the drive with the metal frame attached.



...and removing the hard disk from the frame involves 4 more screws, this are really hard to remove if you don't have the right tools.

Metal frame:
After you get the metal frame, then is the same steps back in reverse oreder :

- Attach the new hard disk to metal frame with the 4 screws
- Insert Hard Disk into Playstation and push to the left with handle to snap it in place.
- Put blue screw back on , and then the plastic latch
- Plug back in and reboot

If everything goes ok, it asks to format the new drive ( yes) and after a while reboot again.
then restore the backup with Saved Games and other goodies and Done!.
