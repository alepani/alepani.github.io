it's fairly complete, except that has no macro support ( thanks god!) and no graphics.
Is google trying to slowly build an office suite, or just to force MS to freeze the file format first ?

Now, let's recap a bit, Gmail, Writely, Google Base (with API), Calendar, search (online and offline), Chat, and Spreadsheet, they are just missing a good presentation/graphic package and there is no reason for the average (say 80% ) of the users to buy or install an office suite, with the added bonus that is available everywhere , cost free, and install free.

Is is posible or desirable for google to develop/buy into a usable online presentation package.
If you check the latest version of Office 12 beta, you can say it's a tough one. Powerpoint is one of the most computer intensive apps in office and the new version is incredible easy to use and can produce stunning results in minutes.

My personal opinion, ( not shared with the majority of people who I have discussions with) is that it will inevitable happen ( Google presenter or whatever they decide to call it) but it should be a couple of years yet before the kind of bandwith / processing needed to do a DHTML/AJAX version of Powerpoint with decent performance are available....
