One day I discovered Bloglines; an online feed reader , it quickly became my reader of choice and never used and off line reader again. The advantages were too much to pass, all my computers synchronized with the news I hadn't read , supporting also various media types ( podcast, etc) and allowing to save post in a folder and/or blog directly from Bloglines.

Bloglines was simple and easy to use, the only problem i have are "growing pains" the downtime of the site has become really a problem ; and the behaviour is erratic: at the time of this writing it's up but won't show my subscriptions.

Fortunately I have my OPML file handy and now I'm trying other online readers, major contenders are Google Reader and Rojo.

I've got two big lessons out of this , nothing new, but makes you think anyway...

1. As more and more of the apps we relay on ( mail , news, photos, documents) are stored online; we must trust companies with important data. Besides privacy issues the reliability of the company becomes a critical issue, here a big-name company ( Google , Ms or Yahoo) gives you more confidence than say Bloglines or a no-name online mail company. This is where acquisitions come in I Trust more in Flickr or Picasa because they where purchased by Yahoo and Google than I would otherwise ( not that I didn't trust before, but it's something positive )

2. Standards . We need open , good/better and reliable standards for our formats; like OPML for feed subs/ maybe Opendoc for documents and some sort of email archiving ( that I am not aware that exists if it does). So in case a service fails or we decide to change from one to another you can be up and running fast.

Ah, and always have a local backup in an external drive with everything you upload.
