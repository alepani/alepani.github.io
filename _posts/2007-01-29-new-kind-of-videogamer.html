--- layout: post title: A new kind of Videogame(r) date: '2007-01-29T14:23:00.000-02:00' author: Alejandro tags: - games - technology modified_time: '2007-03-17T18:54:41.040-03:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-3848561.post-159248435938320605 blogger_orig_url: http://alepanizza.blogspot.com/2007/01/new-kind-of-videogamer.html --- For many people, modern innovation in gaming began with the 3D engine or with the analog controls in the Nintendo64...in some sense this is true; but the innovation we are seeing now it's a different kind, the type which will not only bring better games , more engaging games but games so different from what we ( regular gamers) are used to, that many will not see them as games at all.

The most notable example of this type of thing is Nintendo, and I am not talking only about the Wii controller, wich is in fact innovative.

The NintendoDS is a great example of a different kind of system, but the graphic power is not nearly close as the PSP, yet is has been outselling the PSP for a wide margin in almost every market; the reason: original and great games, it's all it takes.

From "games" where you make numeric tests and sudoku (Brain Age), to games where secret agents must keep the beat of music ( Elite beat agents) or where you make your own music guiding creatures (Elecroplankton) ; to virtual pet games (Nintendogs) where your "puppy" lives in the systems , gets fed, catches a ball and recognize tour voice commands, and even go to other DSes to play with other dogs.
One of several minigames in the new Super Mario makes you blow air into the microphone to lift a character and his balloons.


Of course the Wii is another great example of this type of game in the creative use of a new input device Wii sports it's probably the best executed game , as developers come with new ideas for the remote the Wii will remain a home of new and original titles.

Sony also uses motion control ( in a more limited way ) in the PS3 controller and has not be shown to be of great use at this time, but some games ( most notably Lair ) are coming.

However, the innovation in different games from the Sony software side has been there since PS2:
The EyeToy camera can track your movements ( no remote needed) and map them into the game; the new version works over IP and it's supposed to be better yet. Some PS3 Games will take advantage of this feature to map real object and overlay them in-game ( Eye of Judgement) .
The other interesting game is Singstar Online; it's like a karaoke game only the game check if you hit the pitch correctly and points you for that and you can upload your performance to the Playstation Network. ( think DDR but singing instead of dancing and combine it with myspace )

The third area is letting users create limited games withing the network creating a positive feedback loop ( Microsoft is on that path with "creator's club" in XBox Live)
Sony and Nintendo are also offering downloadable minigames ( not user created yet) but that will come for sure.

This are the kind of games that appeal to people who never touched a console before, the Nintendo strategy ( expanding the market) is the correct one. I hope that the others will follow it.
