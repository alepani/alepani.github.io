--- layout: post title: The future of books ? date: '2007-11-19T17:25:00.000-02:00' author: Alejandro tags: - sony - books - amazon - gadgets - reading - ebooks modified_time: '2007-11-20T14:45:32.220-02:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-3848561.post-410239100258486056 blogger_orig_url: http://alepanizza.blogspot.com/2007/11/future-of-books.html --- Amazon launched today the Kindle eBook reader. Very similar to the Sony's offering, but some things are better, others... not so much.

I've had the Sony PRS-505 for a month now, and I really like it, especially when it comes to reading fiction in native format, ( format conversion is hit or miss , especially with PDF).
The Amazon Kindle ( see the link in the adds :-) ), uses the exact same display technology ( eInk ) which is awesome tech for reading books outdoors, but the Kindle does not connect to a computer, instead, it uses Sprint EV-DO network (called Whispernet) for accessing the Amazon/Kindle store, you can also access Wikipedia (yay !) and update your subscriptions. ( there are no connection fees, so in essence, Amazon is paying your internet bill hoping that you will buy enough books) .


Other niceties : since you have to purchase books from the device, it has a keyboard and a selection wheel, which allows search and annotation in the books. It makes the device bigger and heavier and the connection eats up the amazing eInk battery life, but it's a nice feature.

A couple of things I did not get:

Books are cheaper than Sony connect store, ( 9.99 the average price are they still getting money after paying for EVDO ?) , probably not that's why they charge you for reading blogs , yes that's correct amazon charges you for reading "select blogs" ( they have to be formatted for the device) ; at least Sony Connect blogs ( which offers less than 20 blogs out of millions ) are free.
Why EV-DO this limit's the device to the U.S. and only the U.S. and not WiFi ? which you can get in hotels everywhere for free ?

All in all the devices are functionally equivalent on the reading part, Amazon wireless connectivity helps you if you want to buy a book in a hurry, ( I don't see myself reading blogs in either the Kindle or the Sony, much less browsing, the refresh is too slow and the controls are tuned to linear reading...) , but it kills you if you are outside of Sprint coverage, say Europe, or China.

In conclusion, I think is great that Amazon jumped into digital, I really hope the hardware would work worldwide, but it doesn't. Anyway the books are very aggressively priced and has a big selection (80,000 at least plus magazines and newspapers ) . This will force Sony to wide it's selection and correct the pricing if it wants to stay in the game.

For now , if you travel, read PDFs, or live outside the US , The Sony PRS-505 is the only choice. If you live and work in the US, you have the Kindle/Amazon option... It's at least 100 dollars more but the lower price of books and the connectivity can justify it. Shame it does not have Wifi.....both have to improve a lot to be the "iPod of books"

Update 20/11/07 : Of course the Kidle works outside the US, you just can't connect to Whispernet, ( so you can't purchase books, receive the news or blogs , or use wikipedia ), so it's functionality is very limited.
