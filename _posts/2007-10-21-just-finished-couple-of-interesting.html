Just finished a couple of interesting books:


A whole new mind. Author Dan Pink takes you into a journey to the right side of your brain: why it matters and how to increase your right-brain abilities ( design, empathy, seeing the whole picture, writing stories, etc). I knew the work of Ekman, but the book is worth a read , especially part II ("the six senses").





Daniel Gilbert's "Stumbling on Hapiness" was recommended to me by a friend, and I found it really surprising, If you don't have time to read the book , at least check Gilbert's conference ( there's a link , here ). not only is a good read, but I think that the subject could be developed further into a deeper, more interesting book about the psychology involved.


As soon as Mayda is done with the latest Pinker book, I'm ready...
