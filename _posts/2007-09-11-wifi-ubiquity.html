--- layout: post title: WiFi ubiquity date: '2007-09-11T00:03:00.001-03:00' author: Alejandro tags: - vacations - wireless - networking modified_time: '2008-07-02T14:11:57.787-03:00' thumbnail: http://farm2.static.flickr.com/1218/1355343096_b235fd00a4_t.jpg blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-3848561.post-1942595544094991067 blogger_orig_url: http://alepanizza.blogspot.com/2007/09/wifi-ubiquity.html --- It's crunch-time for us as we near the GX international meeting . The time of the year when we present the new directions in which we are working, and the products that are about to come out from Artech.
Also we have the opportunity to chat informally with the top GX developers who are working in a variety of applications and get feedback about Genexus.

Usually crunch-time means staying late, fast-food and lack of sleep. It still does, but with a twist.

For the past years I was working on Deklarit, I was out of service during summer ( in the south that mens Dec-Jan-Feb) ; and just recently began to help the main developer team with Genexus Rocha so, I'm a bit out of the loop. Still work has to be done, and we have as much as we can handle.

Last weekend was the opportunity to unwind a little so I got the wife and kids to Punta del Este (a.k.a. paradise :-) ), but couldn't help to bring along the laptop to work on my presentation.




We checked four hotels before settling on one and to my surprise three of them had wifi b/g for the guests. On top of that there was no additional charge, login or complications to get on the net, just opened my laptop, saw three APs ( two for the hotel, one for the hotel around the corner) and connected right away.

I personally think this is great, Hotel owners are not stupid, and the more people requested internet access (outside a computer in the lobby), more hotels started to add wifi service.
Punta is a small town and if all of them offered free and open Wifi, the city will be pretty much covered.
