--- layout: post title: Quality time with the OLPC date: '2007-09-22T10:18:00.000-03:00' author: Alejandro tags: - computers - software - gadgets - OLPC modified_time: '2007-10-09T23:53:25.964-02:00' thumbnail: http://farm2.static.flickr.com/1127/1422601565_7d715598a9_t.jpg blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-3848561.post-5130884249704062811 blogger_orig_url: http://alepanizza.blogspot.com/2007/09/quality-time-with-olpc.html --- Yesterday we were able to try one of the new prototypes of the OLPC, ( commonly referred as de $100 dollar computer), from Nicolas Negroponte's One Laptop Per Child project. ( Uruguay's wiki of "Ceibal project")

It'a nice little machine, it looks very solid and rugged.
The OS is a custom flavour of linux (XO) and it has wifi mesh networking built in.
The camera is surprisingly good in quality and apparently can take videos too.

It takes a while to booth but so does my Vista dual core machine :-)



photo by sgomez


On the software side, The Sugar UI is very simple, The Wi-fi utility list machines nearby and connects automatically.
It comes with a word processor, imaging software for the camera, some kind of Logo language, and EToys ( a squeak environment for programming , I already mentioned) and other small utility software.

Overall a it's a nice little system. I hope it serves it purpose ...
