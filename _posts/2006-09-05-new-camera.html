I just got a new camera , my trusty old Sony DSC-P9 (4 Mp 3x) was replaced by a new
Sony Cybershot H2 megazoom camera, a bigger form factor, but it's comfortable enough to carry around.



So far the camera is good, it's not a pro cam but at 6 megapixel with 12x optical zoom, (Carl Zeiss lens), built-in image stabilization, and a small set of manual functions (similar to a SLR), it's very nice.

Te pictures are clean but it has some low-light issues with noise, an a blue-ish tendency, normal on this kind of cameras, nothing unexpected. As I said it's not a SLR ( I would have bough a Sony Alpha 100 or Canon Digital Rebel, if I had an extra $600 :-) )

Also it does 30fps 640x480 video wich is always nice.

I discovered a small problem with high megapixel cameras: they start to make your connection a bottleneck, I upload almost all of my photos to flickr , mostly to share with the family, but also as a backup, I'll have to start choosing pictures because I am now surpassing the flickr monthly limits ( I have three kids )

Overall it's a nice camera. I recommend it.
