When nokia launched de Internet tablet ( Nokia 770 ) , the reviews where not favorable. Apparently the consumers did in fact like it and nokia went out to sell a lot of them.
Recently Microsoft and Intel launched the UMPC initiative, essencially a higher size PDA or a jhalf sized tabled, depending on the way you look at it.

The comparison is not favorable to the UMPC though:
  • Nokia is selling their tablet with WiFi b/g and Bluetooth, card reader a Linux OS with a custom gui, and an open source SDK, for $350
  • Most UMPCs run a special version of Windows with an on screen keyboard, OS comparison aside the cheapest cost 900 dollars and there are some up to $1500.

I know the comparison is not completely fair, because the UMPC has a hard drive and 512 mb of RAM, it's essencially a 7 inch notebook with a slow processor and a keyboard, while the nokia is justa PDA ( 192 MB total, and no HD)

But both devices are targeted at the same public, can play audio and video , and their primary function , I believe, will be wireless browsing/chatting and email. Not running high end apps and you dont need a notebook for that.

Anyway the point is that the Nokia Tablet OS has indeed been upgraded and now includes Google talk ( both text and voice, among other minor interface upgrades)

But than again the same situation happened with MS vs palm at the beggining of the PDA race, and we all know what happened then :-(
