Regardless of the features of IE 7, or the upcoming Firefox version, I have to say they have a lot to do if they want to be my "browser of choice", I have not only installed flock in my three machines and using it for months (home, laptop and work ), but also managed to convince 3 of my co-workers to try it, and they never looked back.

What is so great ?

Nothing much, really , but as they say, the devil is in the details: a Blog editor, with multiple blogs and mutiple engine support, online favorites using delicious and other services, photobucket and flickr uploading/browsing and search fully integrated, search through history, favorites, google, yahoo, etc, . Everything integrated, a fantastic RSS reader .....and support for all the usual favorite firefox extensions : Bugmenot, Greasemonkey, Undoclosetab....

The only thing I dont quite like about the last version is that they require a new window for photo uploading , I kind of preferred the old "developer preview" way.

But If you are tired of adding extensions to firefox, synching favorites, or (god forbid ) still using IE ,check it out

Paul Stamatiou's review and download Flock here.
